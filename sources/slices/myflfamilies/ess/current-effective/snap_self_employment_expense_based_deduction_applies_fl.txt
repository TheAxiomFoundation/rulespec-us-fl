Florida Department of Children and Families, ESS Program Policy Manual,
Chapter 1800: Income, Food Stamps, section 1810.0302 Allowable Costs of
Self-Employment Income:

The assistance group is required to keep a record of the expenses incurred in
the production of self-employment income. Examples of allowable costs of
producing self-employment income are identifiable costs of labor, stock and raw
materials, rent and building maintenance, business telephone costs, vehicle
costs required for the business, principal and interest on loans for income
producing property, insurance premiums and property taxes on income producing
property, child care meal and equipment costs, and travel and lodging away from
home.

Model `snap_self_employment_expense_based_deduction_applies` as whether Florida
allows actual self-employment business expenses to be deducted under
current-effective SNAP policy.
